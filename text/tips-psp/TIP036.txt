#EN#1
The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders%N
A handbook of diagnostic criteria for mental disorders published by the American Psychiatric Association.%N
The title is followed by its revision number.%N
Published editions so far include the%N
DSM-I (1952), DSM-II (1968), DSM-III (1980),%N
DSM-III-R (1987), and DSM-IV(1994).%N
This manual is, so to speak, a "simplified guide to the diagnosis of mental illnesses."%N

#EN#2
Mental illnesses may affect a person in many forms, and are notoriously difficult to diagnose. It is also not uncommon for separate examining doctors to differ in their diagnoses. To help eliminate such errors, the DSM is used as a guide for doctors.%N
By asking the patient certain questions, such as about said patient's condition, and checking their responses against the data in the DSM, even an inexperienced doctor can establish a diagnosis and begin appropriate treatment.%N

#Note: Since the time the story of Remember11 takes place, there have been a few further editions of the DSM: the DSM-IV-TR (2000), an expansion of the DSM-IV with updated diagnostic codes and extra information on each diagnosis, and the upcoming DSM-V (planned for 2012).

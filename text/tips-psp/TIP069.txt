#PARAGRAPHS_TRANSLATED:
#EN#1
In cryptography, the one conveying the information is commonly referred to as Alice, while the recipient is referred to as Bob.%N
This naming convention was first used in a 1978 article presenting the RSA cryptosystem.%N

#EN#2
Additionally, a person found to be intercepting communications (i.e. through a bug or wiretap) between Alice and Bob is customarily referred to as Eve.

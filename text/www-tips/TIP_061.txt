Refers to &quot;Schrödinger's Cat,&quot; a thought experiment
intended as a critique of quantum probability theories
proposed by Bohr and Heisenberg (the so-called Copenhagen interpretation).%N
Suppose that a cat was put in a box equipped with a mechanism
that would emit poisonous gas if a certain radioactive material decays.%P
According to the Copenhagen interpretation,%N
the micro-world phenomena such as radioactive emission
are probabilistic by nature.%N
Therefore, in this experiment, as it is caused by one such phenomenon,
the life/death status of the cat itself ends up being probabilistic as well.%N
This means the cat is both dead and alive at the same time.%N
No certain conclusion to its state can be made until the box is open.%P
The paradox embodied by that cute cat became one of the prime buzzwords
of the era that marked the birth of quantum mechanics.

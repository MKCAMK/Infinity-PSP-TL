（とびとびの値）で表される、物理量の最小単位。

<pre>
The smallest unit of a physical quantity which can be expressed using
values limited to a set of integers within a certain range, rather than continuous values.%N
In 1900, Max Planck proposed the idea of a quantum energy (the quantum hypothesis) that spawned the beginning of quantum theory.%N
Subsequently, Albert Einstein suggested the existence of a light quantum (photon),
and Niels Bohr demonstrated the angular momentum quantum.

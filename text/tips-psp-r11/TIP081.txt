#PARAGRAPHS_TRANSLATED:
#EN#1
In fact, the positions on the Earth's surface such that each of them, when coupled with the two known transfer points, forms the figure which has all of the distances between its vertices in equilibrium, seem to be six.%N
As for where do these positions lie exactly, it is as follows....%N
First, let's designate the surface distance between "point: Aosagi Island" and "point: Mount Akakura" as ｒ.%N

#EN#2
Next, draw two circles of a radius ｒ, centered on "point: Aosagi Island" and "point: Mount Akakura" respectively.%N
Let's designate the intersection points of these two circles as ｐ１ and ｐ２.%N
Draw a line passing through ｐ１ and ｐ２.%N
Also, draw another line passing through "point: Aosagi Island" and "point: Mount Akakura."%N
"Lines" here should be understood in the sense of lying atop a curved surface, and thus forming two more circles around the Earth when fully drawn.%N

#EN#3
With this, we now have two small circles and two big ones.%N
Now,%N
there should be a total of eight points of intersections between all circles——%N
Since two of them are "point: Aosagi Island" and "point: Mount Akakura" we exclude these.%N
Which leaves us with six points——%N
If any of these six represents the actual third area, that would mean that the three transfer points are located in equilibrium positions.

实际上，%N
地球上能保持三点距离均衡的地点有六个。%N
这六个点的位置如下所述：%N
首先，%N
将“点：青鹭岛”与“点：朱仓岳”%N
在球面上的距离设为r，%N
接着分别以“点：青鹭岛”与%N
“点：朱仓岳”为圆心、r为半径，%N
在同一个球面上画出两个圆。%N
将此二圆的两个交点设为p1、p2.%N

#CN#2
画一条直线通过p1、p2.%N
接着再画一条直线通过%N
“点：青鹭岛”与“点：朱仓岳”。%N
这里说的“直线”是指在曲面上的直线，%N
正确地说就是环绕地球一周的圆。%N
此时球面上共有两个大圆与两个小圆。%N

#CN#3
